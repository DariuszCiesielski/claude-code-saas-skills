# Cold Email — Outreach Sequences for SaaS

Generates personalized cold email sequences. From prospect research to follow-up and break-up email.

## When to Use

- Planning a cold email campaign to potential customers
- Want to improve existing templates (low response rate)
- Need a follow-up sequence
- User says: "cold email", "outreach", "write email to client"

## Dependencies

- **Optional:** `growth-lead` — overall acquisition strategy context

## Anatomy of an Effective Cold Email

### Subject Line (opening)
- **Max 6 words** (4-5 ideal)
- **Personalization:** name, company, industry
- **Avoid:** RE:, FWD:, emoji, CAPS, "offer", "partnership"
- **Patterns:** question, observation, mutual connection

### Body (content)
```
[1 sentence — why I'm writing SPECIFICALLY to you]
[1-2 sentences — the problem I solve]
[1 sentence — proof (case study, number)]
[1 sentence — CTA (question, not request)]
```

**Max 80 words.** Every word must work. Shorter = better.

### CTA (call to action)
- **Question** (not request): "Is this a problem for you?" > "Can we talk?"
- **Low commitment:** "15 min" > "meeting"
- **Specific:** "Does Tuesday 10am work?" > "When are you free?"

## 5-Email Sequence (framework)

### Email 1: Initial (Day 0)
**Goal:** Spark curiosity, don't sell

```
Subject: [Observation about prospect's company/industry]

Hi [Name],

[1 sentence — observation about their company/industry, showing research].

[Product] helps companies like [similar company] [measurable result]
— [social proof: "e.g., [Client] saved X hours/week"].

Is [specific pain point] a topic for you?

[Sender name]
```

### Email 2: Value Add (Day 3)
**Goal:** Give value without selling

```
Subject: Re: [subject of email 1]

[Name], quick follow-up.

I wrote a [article/guide/checklist] about [industry problem].
[Link or attachment — REAL value, not an ad].

I think it could be useful given [their situation].

[Sender name]
```

### Email 3: Social Proof (Day 7)
**Goal:** Show that others have this problem and solved it

```
Subject: How [similar company] solved [problem]

[Name],

[Company similar to prospect] had exactly the same problem:
[1 sentence describing the pain].

After implementing [Product]: [measurable result].

Want to see how it works? 15 min demo
tailored to [prospect's industry].

[Sender name]
```

### Email 4: Break-up (Day 14)
**Goal:** Force a decision (yes/no/not now)

```
Subject: Closing the loop?

[Name],

I've written a few times about [problem]. I understand if this isn't
a priority right now.

I want to respect your time — would you say:
a) Interesting topic, but not now (I'll reach out next quarter)
b) Not for me (I'll remove you, no hard feelings)
c) Happy to chat (suggest a time)

[Sender name]
```

### Email 5: Re-engagement (Day 30-60)
**Goal:** Return with new value

```
Subject: [New feature / case study / industry change]

[Name],

Since our last exchange, [Product] added [new feature
relevant to the prospect].

[1 sentence why this is a game-changer for their company].

Worth revisiting?

[Sender name]
```

## Personalization — 3 Levels

| Level | Description | Response Rate | Effort |
|-------|-------------|--------------|--------|
| **L1 — Variable** | {name}, {company}, {industry} | 3-5% | Low |
| **L2 — Segment** | Tailored pain per industry/role | 8-12% | Medium |
| **L3 — 1:1** | Research: LinkedIn post, article, company news | 15-25% | High |

**Recommendation:** L2 for 80% of campaigns, L3 for top 20% of prospects (biggest deals).

### L3 Personalization Sources
- LinkedIn: recent posts, comments, promotion, job change
- Company website: blog, case studies, job openings (indicate priorities)
- Press: funding, partnerships, awards
- Tech stack: BuiltWith, Wappalyzer (if relevant)

## A/B Testing

Test **one variable at a time:**

| What to Test | Variant A | Variant B |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| Subject line | Question | Observation |
| Opening | About them | About the problem |
| CTA | Question | Specific time proposal |
| Length | 50 words | 80 words |
| Social proof | Case study | Metric |
| Tone | Formal | Conversational |

**Min. 50 emails per variant** for statistical significance.

## Anti-patterns (DON'T do)

- "I hope this email finds you well" — wastes 8 words
- "I'm [title] at [company]" at the beginning — nobody cares
- "I'd like to present our offer" — you're selling, not helping
- "Can we schedule a 30-minute presentation?" — too big a commitment
- Attachment in the first email — spam filter
- HTML/images in cold email — looks like a newsletter, not a personal message
- "Dear Sir/Madam" — formality kills response rate
- More than 5 emails in a sequence — harassment
- No unsubscribe — GDPR/CAN-SPAM requires opt-out

## Benchmark Metrics

| Metric | Poor | OK | Good | Great |
|--------|------|-----|------|-------|
| Open rate | <20% | 20-40% | 40-60% | >60% |
| Reply rate | <2% | 2-5% | 5-10% | >10% |
| Positive reply | <1% | 1-3% | 3-5% | >5% |
| Meeting booked | <0.5% | 0.5-2% | 2-5% | >5% |

## Pitfalls

- **Volume ≠ success** — 100 personalized > 1,000 generic
- **Reply ≠ lead** — "I'm not interested" is not a success
- **Timing matters** — Tue-Thu 8-10am = best open rate
- **Warm up domain** — new domain + 1,000 emails right away = blacklist
- **GDPR/CAN-SPAM** — legitimate interest applies to B2B, but always provide opt-out
